FAQs

To be eligible for NDIS psychosocial recovery coaching, individuals must meet the NDIS access requirements and have a psychosocial disability that significantly impacts their daily functioning. The disability must be likely to be permanent and require support for an extended period (typically more than a year).

NDIS Psychosocial Recovery Coaching focuses specifically on assisting individuals with psychosocial disabilities in their recovery journey. It is a specialized form of support that aims to enhance self-determination, build resilience, and promote social inclusion by providing coaching and guidance tailored to individual needs. Support Coordination specifically focuses on assisting participants in coordinating and navigating their supports and services. While other supports provide direct services or therapies, Support Coordination helps individuals understand and access these services effectively to optimize their outcomes.

NDIS Psychosocial Recovery Coaching offers a range of services, including personalized goal setting, development of recovery plans, skill-building exercises, emotional support, practical assistance, and strategies for managing mental health challenges. The coaching sessions are conducted on a one-on-one basis and are designed to empower individuals in their recovery process.

To start accessing NDIS psychosocial recovery coaching, you need to have an approved NDIS plan. If you are eligible for the NDIS and have not yet applied, you can contact the NDIS directly to begin the application process. Once you have an approved plan, you can discuss your interest in psychosocial recovery coaching during your planning meeting or contact the NDIS

Individuals who are approved for the NDIS and have Support Coordination included in their NDIS plan are eligible for this service. Eligibility is based on meeting the NDIS access requirements and having a disability that significantly impacts their daily functioning.

The frequency and duration of NDIS Support Coordination services vary based on individual needs and goals. The NDIS plan specifies the funding allocation for Support Coordination, and the frequency of contact with the support coordinator is determined in agreement with the participant.
